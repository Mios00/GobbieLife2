# GobbieLife — Project Brief (for any new session)

A **Candy Box–style incremental ASCII game**: you play sentient mud that becomes a goblin, then a warren, then a world-conquering (or world-saving) empire. Pure-black terminal aesthetic, JetBrains Mono, no emoji. Three eras, nine stages, five endings.

## Files
- **`GobbieLife Design Bible.dc.html`** — the living design doc: addiction pillars, high-fantasy world + cast + factions, the hybrid narrative engine, live ASCII screens, the 7-tier animated settlement panorama, the building catalog, pacing, and the five-fate pathway map. Read this first for narrative/design intent.
- **`GobbieLife Stage 1 Playable.dc.html`** — the actual playable game, Era I→III end to end (misnamed "Stage 1" historically; it is the whole arc). All systems below live here.
- **`GobbieLife Nav Options.dc.html`** — a decision mockup comparing nav styles (panel filters vs sub-screens). The **hybrid** was chosen and is built.

All three are **Design Components** (`.dc.html`): `<x-dc>` template + `class Component extends DCLogic`. Inline styles only; edit via `dc_write` / `dc_html_str_replace` / `dc_js_str_replace`. Never hand-write `support.js`.

## Resource & economy summary (as built)
- **Era I:** Mud (dig) · Fungus (food). Diggers/Foragers. Breach at 1000 mud.
- **Era II:** Scrap · Blightwood · Ash · Iron · Adamantium · Fungus · Grit · Notoriety. Jobs: Scavengers / Woodcutters / Foragers / Warband(2.3) / idle — the **Job Assignments** panel spans the full center width beside Discovery, jobs laid out as a horizontal cell grid. Buildings (all discovery-gated, cost scales per-build): Char-Kiln, Smelter, **Forge** (with a **Bank/Relight the Forges** toggle — halt Adamantium production to conserve iron+ash), Shanty, Hunting Lodge (**stage-scaled food: +1/+2/+2.5 per lodge at 2.1/2.2/2.3**, +8 comfort), Ash-Bath, **Embassy**(one-time, 380s/140i, reveals Standings + slowly mends relations), Mead Pit, Colosseum(2.2 after Embassy), and two **9-tier upgrade structures** — the **Watchtower** (Wooden Lookout → … → Tier 9 **The Brass Spires**, def 8→240) and the **Wall** (Wooden Stakes → Mud Rampart → Brick → Stone → Iron-Clad → … → Tier 9 **The Iron Horizon**, def 12→380) — each a single structure rebuilt grander per tier (not stacked count); **from Tier 5 both also cost Adamantium** (wall (t−4)×12⬡, tower (t−4)×8⬡) so late defence demands the empire-metal. Plus **The Herald Horn** (formerly the Iron-Clad Bastion — the great work at 2.3, 4× cost: sound it and the world learns the goblins have come; its “note” is the alignment-seeding fork). Discovery is slow (0.2/scavenger); BP_TRACK thresholds Kiln 60 → Horn 1320.
- **Era III:** Adamantium(metal) · Coin · Influence · Food · Unrest · Standings(4 factions). Buildings: Outpost, Trade Route, Granary, **Garrison** (adam — suppresses unrest −3 each) and **Pleasure-Garden** (coin — bread & games, suppresses unrest −2 each **and** warms the Splinter Packs, a coin-vs-adam alternative to the Garrison). Claim territory (Influence+Adamantium, steep scaling). High **Unrest** now also risks an **Uprising** threat — a province revolts; crush it (adam, aggr), buy it back (coin, dipl), or abandon it (−1 land, isol). Single dashboard, no nav tabs. **3.2 The Iron Hegemony = The Congress of Powers:** a **Coalition** meter climbs (∝ hostile-faction count + territories); at 100 the world marches as one host (a severe multi-loss/tribute event, then resets). Four levers bend it and deliberately harden alignment — **Sway**(Inf+Coin→relations+, dipl), **Cow**(Adam→fear/notoriety, aggr), **Seal**(Coin→vanish, isol), **Shield**(Coin+Inf→goodwill, hero). **Below 20% Coalition the world is "at ease"** — a diplomacy dividend of **up to +40% Coin & Influence** (ramps linearly, richest at 0%), a strong pull to keep the meter low. To keep it a live choice, **each lever's resource cost ramps with territory through Era III** (`congressMult` = 1 + max(0, lands−10)×0.03, ≈×1.1 early 3.2 → ×1.63 at 31 lands). Advance to 3.3 at 18 lands **and** alignment locked. **3.3 The Grand Mechanica = the Megaproject build-up, a two-clock race:** **Pour the Hoard** (Adam+Coin+Inf batches) fills the Mechanica bar while **The Dark Beyond** doom-clock climbs 0→100%. It no longer loops — at **every 20% it launches an escalating incursion**: 20% drains resources + minor lives, 40% wrecks infrastructure + outposts, 60% starts **erasing land** (−4 territories/tiles) + medium losses, 80% major infra ruin + heavier land loss (−8), and **100% = the world is swallowed** → instant `hollow_end` bad ending (`hollowStrike`/`hollowEnding`, `hollowStage` 0–5). Finish the Mechanica (100% + themed final-word commit, + Heed the Root) **before** the Dark Beyond crests, or lose everything.
- Six endings + `starved` game-over + **`hollow_end`** (the Dark Beyond devours the world in Era III). Playable at real-time only (no demo speed). Save key `gobbielife_slice_v1`; fate memory `gobbielife_fate_v1`.

## Locked design decisions
- **Tone × Alignment are independent.** Tone (Grimdark ↔ Balanced ↔ Goblin Silly) is **chosen once at the start** and fixed for the run (narrative continuity). Alignment accrues silently and separately.
- **Alignment is HIDDEN** the whole game. The player never sees axis bars. Instead **The Oracle** whispers riddles hinting at the future they're drifting toward. Alignment is only *revealed* at the climax (the themed Megaproject + ending).
- **The Chronicle** (saga log) is exportable/shareable from day one.
- **Six endings:** Villain (aggressive), Goblin Kingdom (isolationist), Melting Pot (diplomatic), Savior (heroic), + **two hidden**: **The Root Rekindled** (tap the Root-Priest glyph in Era III, then “Heed the Root” at the Megaproject) and **The Open Door** (`door` — complete the Chorus of the Open Door questline and choose to sing the last hymn). **The Herald Horn** (the 2.3 great work) seeds alignment by the “note” you sound: **The Grand Maze** (isolation), **The Ashen Conclave** (diplomacy), **The Iron Legion** (aggression), **The Sanctuary** (heroism). Starvation has its own era-scaled `starved` Game Over.
- **Pacing:** a deliberate ~7–8h full run, three plateaus, no easy snowball (coupled upkeep, discovery gating, hard ceilings, Grit/Unrest brakes). Runs in real time only — the former DEMO speed control and stage-jump have been removed for release.

## Systems implemented in the playable
- **Era I — The Feral Awakening** (black/green): scrape mud → recruit Runts (eat Fungus) → Job Assignments balance diggers/foragers → hoard 1000 mud → **Breach the Surface**.
- **Era II — The Iron Hunger** (ember/orange): two raw jobs split the pack — **Scavengers** (Scrap + Discovery) and **Woodcutters** (Blightwood). A full refining chain: **Char-Kiln** (Blightwood→Ash, costs a little Grit as smoke) → **Smelter** (Scrap+Ash→Iron) → **Forge** (Iron+Ash→**Adamantium**, much slower than Iron). Ash is the shared fuel/flux burned by both smelter and forge, so kiln count is a balancing puzzle. Production is deliberately slow (ash 0.6/kiln, iron 0.5/smelter, adam 0.2/forge) and Discovery crawls. Discovery unlocks a **blueprint chain** at rising thresholds (Char-Kiln 70 · Smelter 200 · Hunting Lodge 300 · Ash-Bath 430 · Forge 600 · Mead Pit 820 · Watchtower 1050 · Colosseum 1400). **Population growth (2.2+)** is passive-logistic toward the Housing cap (rate ∝ food surplus × housing headroom, →0 at parity/full; new runts auto-slot the smallest job). The paid **Lure** exists **only at 2.1**. **Comfort** baseline 30; past it, overcrowding bleeds Grit unless offset by Ash-Bath (+10), **Hunting Lodge** (+8 comfort **and stage-scaled food +1/+2/+2.5 per lodge across 2.1/2.2/2.3**), Mead Pit (+15), or the one-time prestige **Colosseum** (+300). Every building's cost **scales with the amount already built** (Kiln < Smelter < Forge; Colosseum is a huge prestige sink). The **Forge** has a **halt toggle** (bank the fires to stop the Adamantium melt and spare iron+ash). Raise **The Herald Horn** (Adamantium) and sound it to ascend into Era III.
- **Era II raids (from 2.3):** random enemy raids scaled by **Notoriety** (population + Adamantium hoard + aggression). Defence = Warband soldiers (+4 each, a job) + the current **Watchtower tier** (8→240) + the current **Wall tier** (12→380); walls/towers from Tier 5 also cost Adamantium. A shortfall between enemy power and defence is multiplied when the gap is large, and the loss is **split between Adamantium (≈78%) and population (≈22%)** — raids can now kill goblins, not just drain metal. Or **pay them off**: the tribute's total **value scales off your Adamantium hoard** and is **randomly split across whatever resources you actually hold** (adam/iron/ash/scrap/blightwood), each priced against Adamantium via an exchange table (1 adam ≈ 6 iron ≈ 10 ash ≈ 12 scrap ≈ 14 wood) so the split equals the demanded value. The hoard that makes you rich is exactly what makes raids deadlier — and pricier to buy off.
- **Settlement Panorama (Eras II–III):** a live 7-tier **wordless** ASCII skyline pinned above the viewport (huddle of huts → palisaded village → market warren → industrial town → fortress-city → city-state → world-capital). Tier is auto-selected from stage + growth (`panoTierFor`), never scrubbed; the header shows only a **size class** (A HUDDLE OF HUTS … A WORLD-CAPITAL — no proper names) + a **live souls count** and a qualitative, tone-aware grandeur description. **Population is derived, not static** (`soulsFor`/`fmtSouls`): Era II scales off the pack (each represented runt stands for a growing multitude — ~160 → ~1.7k → ~8k across 2.1/2.2/2.3), Era III scales off provinces + outposts (`base[stage]·(1 + territories·0.25 + outposts·0.08)`, base 60k/900k/6M) reaching **tens of millions** at the capital (~37M–57M at 3.3). Ported from the Design Bible's panorama.
- **Starvation (all eras):** food deficit → ramping famine (short grace buffer, then deaths accelerate the longer you're short). Random job hit. **Pop 0 = game over** (the `starved` ending) in Eras I–II. In Era III famine instead starves **Outposts** off the map.
- **Era III — The World Blight** (brutalist amber): **Adamantium** is the empire's metal (Outposts produce Adamantium + Coin). Outposts eat **super-linear Food** (`0.4·outposts^1.4`) **plus** a per-land cost (`0.5·territories`); **Granary** burns Coin→Food. **Claiming land** costs escalate (×1.25/claim) to model an empire slowing under its own weight. Territory map fills toward 31, **Unrest** brake (suppressed by Garrisons **and** Pleasure-Gardens; high unrest can trigger an **Uprising** threat that costs a province), **The Standings** (four-faction diplomacy) drive war/disaster/Dark-Beyond threats, hidden alignment → **Megaproject** → branching ending. Nav is a single calm dashboard (LOG + OUTPOSTS filters removed; the Chronicle is always visible).
- **Diplomacy engine.** *Era II* = **Notoriety** + **The Standings** once you build an **Embassy** (from 2.2): the four-faction relations become visible and the Embassy slowly mends them, while raids sour the raiding faction. *Era III* = **The Standings** in full: four factions (**The Sunspire**/elves, **The Iron States**/humans, **The Dwarrow Holds**/dwarves, **The Splinter Packs**/goblins), each −100..+100. Reckoning choices shift them (aggressive lowers, diplomatic raises — auto-mapped from the alignment tilt of each choice, plus explicit shifts on threat events). A faction at ≤−30 is **hostile** and periodically **marches on you** (a War threat losing Adamantium/Coin, or peace bought with tribute + restored relations). A separate **Threat timer** rolls War / Natural Disaster (severity scales with outpost count) / **The Dark Beyond** (the Hollow — impact and grandeur scale up as you near the endgame). Relations surface in a **STANDINGS** panel.
- **Attitude-shift events** (`attitudeEvent` + `attitudeStems`, kind **`THE STANDINGS`**): a combinatorial faction-relations generator for Eras II–III. Each of the four powers (Sunspire/Iron States/Dwarrow Holds/Splinter Packs) has **9 scenario stems** — 4 era-II + 5 era-III (≥3 per faction per faction-era) — and every event assembles a stem + a **warm** (courted) and **cold** (defiant) choice with **randomised outcome flavor** and a **randomised relation magnitude** (±6..12), plus a small era-appropriate resource nudge (warm costs scrap/coin, cold gains it). Era-II events gate to stage 2.2+ (humans only from 2.3). Fired from `rollEvent` at ~20% in Eras II–III, so they interleave with ordinary Reckonings. (Factions do not exist in Era I — no attitude events there.)
- **Faction questlines** (`factionQuest`/`factionArc`/`fqStep`+`fqStance`, persisted): each of the four powers has a **4-step arc threaded across eras that remembers prior choices**. Step 1 opens in Era II (elves/dwarves 2.2, humans/splinters 2.3), step 2 in Era III, step 3 in 3.2+, and a **step-4 capstone in 3.3** (The Black Arrow / The United Front / Korrin's Final Ledger / The Last Horde) that pays off the whole stance history at the end of the world. Every step offers a **defiant / courted / aloof** choice that sets a stored `fqStance`; the next step's title, body and result branch on that stance (e.g. defy the Sunspire → step 2 "The Ridge of Spears"; court them → "Aelith in Person"). Finales pay off big relation/alignment swings + coalition shifts (Sunspire Splits, Human Reckoning, Monopoly of Roads, The Goblin Question). Fired via `maybeFactionOrEvent` (~45% of a normal event slot when a step is due), so they interleave with ordinary Reckonings.
- **Risk/reward event outcomes** (`gamble` on a choice): some Reckoning choices resolve to a random **good/bad** branch (`ch.gamble` = P(good); resolveEvent merges `ch.good`/`ch.bad` before applying — top-level `dAlign`/`dRel` on the choice persist across both branches). **~20 gamble events, ≈15–16% of the Reckoning pool** across all three eras (e.g. Keep Digging, Follow the Echo, Dig Toward It, Overseer's Whip, Scrap Storm, Burst Boiler, Caravan Raid, Collapsed Armory, Gas Pocket, Strange Engine, Prodigy Tinker, Grey Rot, Foreign Assassin, Comet, Crusade, Great Drought, Mercenary Legion, Mountain Pass, Border Kingdom, The Flood). Many library choices also carry **faction-attitude shifts** (`dRel`, and dual-faction `dRel2`) keyed to the response.
- **The Oracle** (`oracleBank` + `oracleExtra`, ~116 riddles across moods `unformed / faint / clear`): refreshes slowly (~every 48–84 ticks) so each line lands; occasionally logged to the Chronicle. Replaces all visible alignment UI.
- **The Hollow** (`hollowWhispers` + `hollowEvents`): rare mythic lore — ancient tablets, breathing doors, welcome-friezes — hinting a greater story behind the mist. Whispers (~6% roll) and 3 discovery events begin at **2.3** and run through Era III; both are logged with the `hollow` kind and shown in a distinct cold-cyan colour (◍ tag). Ties toward the heroic/Root fates (small `alHero` nudges).
- **The Charge** (`questsFor`): poetic per-stage quest objectives with live progress (e.g. "Hoard a thousand loads of mud, then breach the roof of the world" 640/1000). Tone-aware (silly variants).
- **Events / Reckonings** (`rollEvent` + `eventsExtra`, **~130 total, ~39/42/47 per era** after the +50% growth pass): era-scaled choices (warren survival → industrial → geopolitical), fire ~every **75–150 ticks** (widened ~10%: Era I 75+rand57, Era II 79+rand57, Era III 88+rand62). **Calamities doubled to ~22** (CALAMITY/PLAGUE/FAMINE-kind, Era-weighted toward II & III, escalating in scale — cave-in → killing smog / tailings burst / ash-winter → great quake / blood flux / starfall / year-without-summer / Hollow tide; several bodies assembled from inline fragment picks for freshness). Choices move resources and silently tip alignment. Includes named cast (Aelith, Sylvareth, the Tallyman, Grosh, Korrin, the Root-Priest, the Hollow).
- **Panel-filter navigation (all eras):** nav tabs are lightweight **filters over one calm dashboard**, never separate screens. The first tab is the overview (everything visible) and the rest narrow focus — Era I: WARREN=all / PACK=jobs / STORES=build / LOG=chronicle; Era II: SURFACE=all / WORKS / JOBS / LOG; Era III: DOMINION=all / OUTPOSTS / LOG. The viewport "stage" and the Charge/Oracle rail stay put; the Chronicle shows on the overview and its own LOG filter. (An earlier hybrid that turned Eras II–III into sub-screen "rooms" was tried and **reverted** — panel filters everywhere is the decision. `GobbieLife Nav Options.dc.html` documents the comparison; option 1A won.)
- **Stage names** (serious + Goblin-Silly variants via `stageName(st, tone)`):
  1.1 The Lone Scrapper / A Very Lonely Goblin · 1.2 The Runt Pack / The Little Gremlin Gang · 1.3 The Mud Warrens / The Squelchy Burrows · 2.1 The Smog-Pit / The Stinky Hole · 2.2 Smoghaven / Stinkytown · 2.3 The Ferrous Vault / The Shiny Metal Fort · 3.1 The Ashen Dominion / The Sooty Kingdom · 3.2 The Iron Hegemony / The Big Bossy Empire · 3.3 The Grand Mechanica / The Giant Doohickey.
- **Juice:** full-screen milestone flashes on stage-ups, floating "+N" click pops, pulsing bottleneck/Megaproject buttons. Autosave + capped offline progress to `localStorage` key `gobbielife_slice_v1`.

## The Wander, the Chorus, and the release systems (July 2026 — built)
- **The Wander (adventure mode)** (`ADVENTURES` + `ADV_B`/`ADV_C`/`ADV_D` pools, `advStep`/`adv`/`advCd`/`advPerks` persisted): a parallel 9-quest chain in the side column, one wanderer = **the player** (see named player below), stage-gated 1.2→3.3. **Each of the 9 slots has 4 flavour variants** (36 quests total) sharing the same lore beat + reward; the per-run variant is seed-stable (`advPickIdx`). Each journey is a 5-beat mini-story: departure → a random road fortune at 25% (`ROAD_GOOD`/`ROAD_BAD` ±12% reward) → an **interactive encounter at 50%** → another random beat at 75% → **graded homecoming** (all modifiers stack into `adv.mod`). Encounters are one of: a **thematic choice** (fires as a `THE WANDER` event, `wander:true`, violet card), or a **minigame** — **riddle** (rated on correctness), **speed** (8s mash, rated on count), or **memory** (hum the hymn back, rated on how far you got). Rewards: mud/fungus/scrap/iron/adam/coin/influence/grit, relics (`lore`), faction relations, alignment, `−hollowRise`, and the permanent **wardstones** perk (+12 defence, −2 unrest). The Wander travel pauses while a `wander` event is open. Log kind **`wander`** (violet ⚑).
- **The Chorus of the Open Door** (Hollow cult): surfaces at **2.2** and escalates through **3.3** in three phases (`cultPhase`): clandestine (chalk circles, unison humming) → open (slag-heap shrines, street preachers, vanishing workers) → brazen (mass processions, flesh-carving, portal-widening rites). ~30 tone-aware whispers (log kind **`cult`**, violet ◉) + 6 choice events (`cultEvent`), and in Era III those choices move the Dark Beyond doom-clock (`dHollow`). A **4-step stance-branching questline** (`cultQuest`/`cultStep`/`cultStance`): The First Sermon (2.3) → The Patient Door (3.1) → The Offer (3.2) → **The Last Hymn** (3.3 capstone). The capstone lets you end the Chorus forever **or sing with them** → the **sixth ending `door` (The Open Door)** via a unique Final-Night screen. The cult also gives the randomized portal land-loss its fiction (“portals open where the hymn was thickest”).
- **Named player** (`pname`, `nameFor(tone)`): the goblin is given a randomized, tone-flavoured name at the voice-choice screen (Grimdark / Balanced / Goblin-Silly banks), announced as a full-screen flash + saga line, shown in the top bar, and used throughout The Wander.
- **New-Game+ keepsakes** (`keepsakeFor()` off `FATE_KEY`): each remembered ending grants one small boon to the next run (Villain → +1 def/soldier; Melting Pot → warmer starting relations; Goblin Kingdom → bigger mud-hoard; Savior → Hollow speaks a stage earlier; Root → one relic pre-known; Door → Hollow whispers early; starved/hollow → starting fungus). Shown as an intro parchment card + Chronicle line.
- **Cinematic ending** (`renderVals` ending block): slow-reveal sequence — tag → sigil → title → body → **FROM YOUR CHRONICLE** quotes → **THE SAGA'S MEASURE** (days, souls, lands, relics, mud scraped, roads wandered, voice) → **HOW THE WORLD REMEMBERS YOU** (per-faction epitaphs from final relations + remembered questline stance) → **THE FINAL RECKONINGS** (completed faction-finale epilogue paragraphs) → the Oracle keeping your fate. Each beat fades in on its own timing (`endReveal`).
- **The Final Night** (`finalNight`): before the endgame resolves (Mechanica / Root / Door), a hushed one-screen pause where the Oracle speaks plainly, tone- and alignment-aware, then “FACE WHAT COMES →” into the ending.
- **Hex world map + randomized portal loss** (`worldMap(seed)` memoized): Era III territory is a seeded Catan-style cluster of named hexes (`nameFor` land names). Dark Beyond incursions open **portals at random** (`destroyedIdx`) — claimed lands first — leaving cold void scars; destroyed lands are permanent and shrink the reclaimable cap.
- **Build-queue timer** (`BUILD_KEYS`/`beginBuild`/`advanceBuilds`/`buildDef`/`bbtn`): every building/infrastructure pays its cost up front then constructs over a cost-scaled duration (~3–24s), the button itself a live progress bar. Persisted in `builds`.
- **Era III saga tentpoles** (`s3f`): five once-per-run narrative beats (first outpost, ten lands, coalition forms, Dark Beyond half-sky, Mechanica half-built).
- **Chronicle export**: downloads a styled standalone HTML page (colour-coded by log kind, voice/stage/fate header) plus plain text to clipboard.

## Performance architecture (do not regress)
The per-second sim runs in **ONE batched `setState`** (`tick()` → pure `_eraI/_eraII/_eraIII(s)` returning patches, with `ticks` + oracle countdown folded in). `advanceBuilds`/`advanceAdventure`/`checkStage` early-return `null` when idle so they add no render. **Autosave is throttled** (`save()` dirty-flag + `saveNow()` at most every `SAVE_MS`=5s) with forced writes on stage-up, endings, and `visibilitychange`/`pagehide`. The **hex map is Era-III-only and memoized** on `(worldSeed, territories, effMax, destroyedIdx)`. The **Chronicle typewriter** reveals 4 chars/45ms. Measured churn ≈6 DOM mutations/3s in steady play. See `OPTIMIZATION_PLAN.md`. Known open items in `BUG_REVIEW.md` (Wander-encounter-vs-open-event guard is the one real fix pending).

## Conventions
- **Whispers engine** (`rollWhisper`): layered — mostly combinatorial (`bankWhisperParts`, era-scaled who/did/what/where, **~18 each per tone × era**) plus authored **stage-specific** lines (`stageWhispers`, 1.1→3.3, **~8 per stage per tone → 144 total** after the doubling pass), **cast/faction** lines (`castWhispers`, named cast + factions, from 2.2, **84 total**: 26 serious + 16 silly per era-bucket), and rare **Hollow** lines (`hollowWhispers`, from 2.3, **64 total**: ~40 serious / ~24 silly). All banks carry grim/mid/silly variants. Era-separated; taper (`whisperEra`) bleeds a little prior-era flavor into each era's opening stage (`x.1`, ~40%), then only the current era's imagery from `x.2` on. Logged with the returned `kind` (whisper vs hollow).
- Aesthetic: pure black (#000) base; Era palettes ash-green → ember-orange → brutalist-amber. Crisp JetBrains Mono. ASCII/Unicode block art (`█▓▒░║`). No emoji, no gradient slop.
- Narration voice bends with Tone everywhere (scrape lines, whispers, saga beats, quests, some events). The **silly tone** carries a heightened comic register — punchy, on-the-nose, gently pop-culture-flavored — across all new and recurring silly lines (scrape/forage/scavenge/muster/recruit banks, intro/surface/dominion flavor, cold-open, saga beats, event bodies, whispers, calamities).
- When adding events/riddles, keep them **era-scaled** (what goblins care about at that point) and add variety to avoid staleness.

## Addiction & worldbuilding pass (July 2026 — built)
- **Relics of the Before** (`LORE_TRACK` 12 thresholds 40→1240 + `loreBank`, log kind `lore`, parchment ▣ #c9b271): Era II Discovery now surfaces a 12-part lore chain between blueprints telling the world's backstory in order — the two-sun empire, the Sundering war-map (with the forgotten goblin doodle), the World-Tree god, goblins as its root-tenders, adamantium as the god-killing metal, ash/blightwood origins, the god's body as a door over **the Hollow**, the Concord of Ash (the powers' pact of forgetting), the seeress prophecy, the ancients' unfinished Mechanica, and the root-tender's confession (Root-ending foreshadow). `loreFound` persisted.
- **Uzgar Red-Maw** (`uzDue`/`uzgarQuest`/`uzgarArc`, state `uzStep`/`uzStance`, kind `THE RED MAW`): a named recurring antagonist — warlord of the ash-wastes — with a 4-step stance-branching arc (2.1 toll → 2.2 escalation → 2.3 he comes himself → Era III finale: warlord's death / march-lord's boon / the driverless cart). Fired from `maybeFactionOrEvent` (~35% when due, checked before faction quests).
- **Ending epigraphs**: `save()` harvests every `saga` log line into a persistent `marks` list (cap 40, dedup); the ending screen shows 2–3 of them as "FROM YOUR CHRONICLE" quotes, so each ending replays the run's own beats.
- **NEXT strip** (`nextGoals()` + side-column panel above The Charge): always shows 1–2 almost-reachable goals with progress bar + live ETA per era (next blueprint, stage milestone, breach, herald horn, claim-a-land, mechanica). ETA from current rates; 'ready!' when affordable.
- **Raid/threat early warnings**: 2.3 shows "⛨ RAIDERS SIGHTED — at the walls in ~Ns · defence X" when `nextRaid ≤ 30`; Era III shows a couriers warning when `nextThreat ≤ 30` — an appointment mechanic (time to reassign the Warband / stock coin).
- **Scaling active verbs**: SCAVENGE SURFACE click gains `+⌊scavengers×0.25⌋` scrap / `+⌊woodcutters×0.25⌋` wood on top of base; MUSTER gains `+⌊territories×0.4⌋` influence / `+⌊routes×0.5⌋` coin — clicking stays relevant all game.
- **2.1 micro-milestone**: after the first kiln, a Charge task "Bank thirty ash against the smelter to come" bridges the kiln→smelter discovery gap.
- **Fate-memory Oracle** (`FATE_KEY` = `gobbielife_fate_v1`): each ending is remembered; the next run's Oracle opens with a riddle referencing the last fate ("Last time the tale ended in fire…") — a cheap New-Game+ pull.
