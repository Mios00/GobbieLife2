The nav tabs: two ways to make them real

Right now the tabs (SURFACE / SCAVENGE / WORKS / BARTER) are decorative. Here are the two live options, mocked with the Era II interface. Tell me 1a or 1b.

Both keep the top resource bar and the tabs exactly where they are — the difference is what a tab-click does.

1A

Panel Filters

A tab focuses the same screen — the viewport stays put and the tab decides which side-panels are in view. Lightweight; nothing is hidden far away. Good on small screens, where all panels at once get cramped.

▲ STRENGTHS
Nothing buried; fast to read. Declutters a busy screen. Cheap to build. The world never "leaves."
▼ TRADE-OFFS
Less room for each system to grow deep. Tabs feel like toggles, not places. Limited spectacle.
Best if you want the game to stay a single calm dashboard that you tidy as it grows.
1B

Sub-Screens

A tab travels to a dedicated room — the whole main area swaps to a full screen built for that system (a real pipeline board, a war map, a market floor). More depth, more spectacle, a stronger sense of place.

Best if you want each era's core system to feel like a destination you visit and master.
✓ DECIDED — 1A · PANEL FILTERS (all eras)  ·  the hybrid was tried, then reverted

Start with 1A panel-filters in Era I (one calm screen, nothing to hunt for), then let the tabs "graduate" into 1B sub-screens in Eras II–III, exactly where the systems earn a room of their own (the pipeline floor, the war map). The UI growing from filters → destinations becomes one more way the interface levels up with you. But if you want a single answer to build first, pick one and I'll wire it.
